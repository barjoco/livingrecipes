Egyptian Beetroot Salad & Machshi Stuffed Vegetable and Rice

Vegitarien dish for 2 people or accompanying salad for 4 people.

(As an accompanying salad excellent with grilled chicken or fish)

Ingredients

  • 2 Medium Beetroots Cooked without Vinegar or Raw. If cooking, boil for an hour in water.
  • 1 Large Garlic Clove
  • Chopped Coriander
  • Lemon Juice
  • Olive Oil
  • Cumin
  • Salt and Pepper

Method

Slice/Cube the Beetroot and Mix all the Rest of the Ingredients Together


Machshi Stuffed Vegetable and Rice Served 2

Ingredients

Rice

  • 1 Onion Finley Chopped and Caramelised
  • 3 Cloves of Garlic Finely Chopped
  • 2 Peppers or 2 Pale Courgette
  • 1 cup Chopped Dill, Parsley, Coriander
  • 1⁄2 Tsp Turmeric, Cumin, Ground Coriander
  • Sea Salt, Coarse Black Pepper

Sauce

  • 120g Tomato Paste
  • Water
  • Turmeric, Cumin, Ground Coriander
  • Sea Salt, Coarse Black Pepper

Method

  1. Core the Peppers/ Courgette
  2. Mix all the Rice Ingredients together and stuff the vegetables until you reach half full
  3. Place all the ingredients in bowl and whisk until Mixed
  4. Fit all the vegetables in a pan and pour the sauce in to the pan (Do not pour on vegetables directly)
  5. Bring to a gentle simmer and cook for 40 minutes on a low heat

Seared Yemeni Chickpeas & Fresh Crispy Vegetables

(Serves 1 to 2 as a full meal or a family as an accompaniment)

Ingredients

Dry

  • 1 tin of chickpeas – drained, washed and dried
  • Yemeni spice mix jar 1 & 2

Fresh

  • 1 Garlic clove – peeled and finely diced
  • 1 Baby cucumber – washed and diced
  • 2 Tomatoes – washed and diced
  • 1 Pepper – washed and diced
  • 1 Shallot – peeled and finely diced
  • 1 Lemon – Zested and juiced

Living Herbs

  • Flat leaf Parsley – finely chopped including stalks
  • Coriander – finely chopped including stalks
  • Mint – leaves picked and finely chopped
  • Dill – finely chopped including stalks
  • Watercress – serve as a side salad

From your pantry

  • 6 tbsp olive oil – for frying and dressing
  • 1⁄4 tsp salt
  • 1⁄4 tesp coarse black pepper

Method

  1. Heat a large pan with 3 tbsp of olive oil. While the pan is heating up mix the chickpeas with the Yemeni spice mix 1 and 2, and half the salt and pepper
  2. Fry the chickpeas until golden brown, then set aside to cool
  3. Once cooled, add the diced fresh vegetables and leave to cool to room temperature
  4. Add the remaining 2 tbsp of olive oil, lemon juice and zest, remaining salt and pepper and mix well
  5. Add the freshly chopped herbs and mix
  6. Serve in a nice deep bowl
  7. Garnish with watercress
